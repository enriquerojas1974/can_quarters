The 1970s

Pure Nickel Era — RCMP Centennial — The Legendary Large Bust

The 1970s were Canada's first full decade of pure nickel quarters. Production volumes swelled to meet growing commercial demand. The decade's numismatic highlight is the 1973 RCMP Centennial commemorative — and hidden within that issue, the Large Bust variety, caused by an accidental use of a 50-cent obverse hub, making it one of the most sought-after modern Canadian rarities.

1973 RCMP Centennial Commemorative • Two Obverse Varieties

1973SB

1973 RCMP Centennial — Small Bust Commemorative

197399.9% Nickel

Commemorates the 100th anniversary of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police. Reverse by Paul Cederberg shows a mounted officer in dress uniform riding right. The Small Bust is the standard Machin tiara portrait and accounts for virtually all of the mintage.

Mintage: ~135,948,000 of the combined 135,958,589 total

1973LB

1973 RCMP Centennial — Large Bust Commemorative

197399.9% Nickel

The most famous modern Canadian variety. A 50-cent piece obverse hub was accidentally used, producing a noticeably larger portrait of the Queen. Estimated fewer than 10,000 examples exist in all grades. Same RCMP reverse as the Small Bust issue.

Mintage: ~10,000 estimated (included in 135,958,589 total)

★ Key Date / Variety — Values range from $150 (VF) to $1,200+ (MS-65). Easily identified by the noticeably larger Queen's portrait relative to the rim.
