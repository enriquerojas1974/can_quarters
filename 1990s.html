The 1990s

New Portrait • 1991 Key Date • 1992 Provinces Series • 1999 Millennium

The 1990s are the most eventful decade in Canadian quarter history. They open with Dora de Pedery-Hunt's crowned portrait in 1990 and immediately deliver the 1991 key date — the lowest business-strike mintage of the post-WWII era at just 459,000 pieces. They continue with the 1992 Canada 125 / Provinces & Territories series (12 designs, one per month), the first multi-coin circulating commemorative program in Canadian history. The decade closes with the legendary 1999 Millennium series — 12 more designs — and sees 1997 and 1998 produce no circulation quarters at all.

1990 — New Portrait Debut

199099.9% Nickel

First year of Dora de Pedery-Hunt's crowned portrait — the first Canadian coin portrait of the Queen by a Canadian artist. She is shown wearing the Girls of Great Britain and Ireland tiara. Caribou reverse unchanged.

Mintage: 31,258,000

1991

1991 — The Great Key Date

199199.9% Nickel

The rarest business-strike Canadian quarter of the post-WWII era. Only 459,000 struck for circulation — a fraction of any typical year. The RCM had massive coin stocks in reserve and simply didn't need to produce many. Standard caribou reverse, De Pedery-Hunt portrait.

Mintage: 459,000

★ Extreme Key Date — Highly sought by collectors. Worth significantly above face value even in worn condition. Estimate $10–$35 in VF; $80–$300+ in Mint State.

1992 Canada 125 — Provinces & Territories Series 12 Designs • "1867–1992" Dual Date

The first multi-coin circulating commemorative program in Canadian history, anticipating the U.S. 50 State Quarters by six years. Released one design per month throughout 1992. All use the De Pedery-Hunt obverse with dual dates "1867–1992". Combined mintage: approximately 153 million.

1997 — No Circulation Strikes

1997

No quarters were produced for general circulation in 1997. Existing stockpiles from 1995–96 met all commercial demand. Proof and specimen versions were produced for collector sets only.

Mintage: 0 (circulation)

1998—

1998 — No Circulation Strikes

1998

A second year of no circulation production. Collector-only silver commemoratives (92.5% Ag, dated 1998) were issued but not for commerce. The gap set up massive demand heading into 1999.

Mintage: 0 (circulation)

1999 Millennium — "A Country Unfolds" 12 Designs • De Pedery-Hunt Portrait

Twelve different reverses released monthly throughout 1999, all dated 1999. The series looks back at defining chapters of Canadian history. Total combined mintage: ~263 million. De Pedery-Hunt crowned portrait on all obverses. All in pure nickel.
