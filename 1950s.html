The 1950s

First Coins of the Reign — Silver Caribou, Gillick Portrait

The first Canadian quarters of Queen Elizabeth II's reign were struck in 80% silver / 20% copper, continuing the alloy Canada had used since 1920. The obverse bears the laureate portrait by British sculptor Mary Gillick. Two varieties exist for 1953: the No Shoulder Fold (NSF) — the original master die — and the Shoulder Fold (SF), a mid-year correction where engravers strengthened the detail on the Queen's gown at the shoulder. The reverse retains Emanuel Hahn's iconic caribou head introduced in 1937.

Standard Caribou Design Obv: Gillick Laureate • Rev: Caribou Head • 80% Silver

1953NSF

1953 — No Shoulder Fold (NSF)

1953 80% Silver

The original 1953 master die. The Queen's shoulder and hairline show less relief; some water lines on the obverse are also absent or faint. First year of the Elizabeth II quarter.

Mintage: 10,456,769 combined (NSF + SF)

NSF and SF share the total mintage; NSF is the less common variety in higher grades.

1953SF

1953 — Shoulder Fold (SF)

1953 80% Silver

Mid-year modification: engravers added a fold of fabric at the shoulder and sharpened the hairline. Considered the "improved" die and used for all subsequent years. More commonly found in circulation.

Mintage: Included in 10,456,769 total

1954

1954

1954 80% Silver

Lowest mintage of the 1950s. Shoulder Fold obverse only. Standard caribou reverse.

Mintage: 2,318,891

Low production reflects ample coin supply from 1953 striking. More scarce in choice condition.

1955DDO

1955 — incl. Doubled-Die Obverse Variety

1955 80% Silver

Regular issue plus a notable doubled-die obverse variety where doubling is visible on the legend and portrait. The doubled-die is a key variety for collectors.

Mintage: 9,552,505

★ Key variety: 1955 Doubled-Die Obverse — look for doubling on "ELIZABETH II" and portrait details.
