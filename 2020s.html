The Final Years — Queen Elizabeth II Passes — September 8, 2022

The final three years of Queen Elizabeth II's coinage are the quietest in terms of commemoratives for the quarter denomination. The COVID-19 pandemic affected production planning and coin demand, while no special circulation commemorative 25-cent pieces were issued for the 2020–2022 period. All coins carry Susanna Blunt's uncrowned portrait, the RCM logo, and multi-ply plated steel composition. Queen Elizabeth II passed away on September 8, 2022, at Balmoral Castle, aged 96, ending the reign depicted on every coin in this directory. The 2022 dated quarter is the last of her reign; King Charles III now appears on all new Canadian coinage.

2022

2022 — Standard Caribou (Final Elizabeth II Issue)

2022Plated Steel

The last circulation quarter bearing the portrait of Queen Elizabeth II. Struck before and after the Queen's passing on September 8, 2022. No commemorative circulation quarter was issued in 2022 for the Queen's Platinum Jubilee or her passing — those tributes were reserved for collector-grade silver and gold pieces. This plain caribou issue is the final chapter of a 70-year numismatic story.

Mintage: ~91,680,000

Historic last year. The first post-Elizabeth II Canadian quarter, bearing King Charles III's portrait, was introduced in 2023.

Queen Elizabeth II — 1926–2022

Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II appeared on Canadian coins from 1953 to 2022 — a span of nearly seven decades. Across that time, her portrait evolved through four distinct effigies by four different artists: Mary Gillick, Arnold Machin, Dora de Pedery-Hunt, and Susanna Blunt. More than nine billion Canadian quarters bearing her likeness were struck during her reign — more than any other denomination. Each one carries a small piece of Canadian history.
