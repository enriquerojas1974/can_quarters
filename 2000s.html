The 2000s

Millennium Dreams • Plated Steel • New Portrait • Vancouver Olympics

The 2000s saw the most rapid design changes in Canadian quarter history. The decade opened with 12 more Millennium designs (the "Dreams for the Future" series) and introduced multi-ply nickel-plated steel planchets in 2001, marked by a "P" on the obverse. In 2003, Susanna Blunt's uncrowned portrait debuted. Coloured circulation coins arrived in 2004. And from 2007, the decade closed with the sprawling Vancouver 2010 Winter Olympics sport-design series.

2000 Millennium — "Dreams for the Future" 12 Designs • Nickel • De Pedery-Hunt Portrait

The companion series to 1999, now looking forward rather than back. Twelve designs, one per month, all dated 2000. Total combined mintage approximately 415,894,000. Note: A tiny handful of "P" (plated steel) test pieces were struck for November ("Freedom") and December ("Community") — the 2000P test tokens are among the rarest Canadian coins, with only 2–5 known of each.

Annual Issues & Commemoratives 2001–2006 • Plated Steel Era Begins

2001P

2001 — Standard Caribou (First "P" Year)

2001Plated Steel

First year of multi-ply nickel-plated steel for all circulation quarters. A small "P" appears below the De Pedery-Hunt portrait to denote the new composition. Hahn caribou reverse. The coins are slightly lighter (4.4 g vs 5.07 g).

Mintage: 62,227,000

2002GJ

2002 — Golden Jubilee & Standard Caribou Jubilee

1952–2002Plated Steel

Standard caribou reverse with the dual jubilee dates "1952–2002" on the obverse, marking the Queen's 50th year on the throne. A separate Canada Day maple leaf commemorative (by Judith Chartier) was distributed at citizenship ceremonies: mintage ~30,627,000.

Mintage: 156,105,000 (standard) + ~30,627,000 (Canada Day)

2003P

2003 — New Blunt Portrait Debut

2003Plated Steel

Transition year with both the De Pedery-Hunt crowned portrait and Susanna Blunt's uncrowned portrait in use. Blunt's portrait shows an older, uncrowned Queen — the first portrait without a crown since the Gillick laureate. "P" mintmark continues.

Mintage: 100,638,000 (combined both portrait varieties)

2004

2004 — Acadia 400th & Remembrance Poppy Comm.

2004Plated Steel

Two major commemoratives: (1) Acadia / Île Ste-Croix 400th Anniversary (R.R. Carmichael, ship "La Bonne-Renommée"): 15,400,000; (2) Remembrance Poppy (Cosme Saffioti) — world's first coloured circulation coin: 28,500,000. Standard caribou also issued.

Mintage: ~166,147,000 total (all types)

2005

2005 — Veterans, Alberta & Saskatchewan Centennials Comm.

2005Plated Steel

Three commemoratives issued alongside standard caribou: (1) Year of the Veteran (Elaine Goble): 29,390,000; (2) Alberta Centennial (Michelle Grant): 20,640,000; (3) Saskatchewan Centennial with western meadowlark (Paulette Sapergia): 19,290,000.

Mintage: ~209,866,000 total (all types)

2006

2006 — Pink Ribbon & Medal of Bravery Comm.

2006Plated Steel

Two commemoratives: (1) Pink Ribbon — Breast Cancer Awareness (coloured, Cosme Saffioti): 29,798,000; (2) Medal of Bravery (RCM Engravers): 20,040,000. The RCM logo replaces the "P" mintmark from this year forward.

Mintage: ~423,189,000 total (all types)

Vancouver 2010 Winter Olympics Series 2007–2010 • 15 Sport Designs • Blunt Portrait

Fifteen different sport/event reverses released over three years to promote the 2010 Vancouver Winter Olympics and Paralympics. Each standard sport design: 22,400,000. The 2009–2010 "Golden Moment" pairs were ~19,000,000 plain + ~3,000,000 colourized each, inserted randomly into coin rolls. All use Blunt uncrowned portrait.
