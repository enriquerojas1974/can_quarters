The 1960s

Silver's Last Stand — Centennial Commemorative — Transition to Nickel

Back to all decades

The 1960s opened with 80% silver quarters surging to record production volumes. By 1964, the Gillick laureate portrait gave way to Arnold Machin's tiara portrait. Rising silver prices forced a composition change: the 1967 Centennial year saw two alloys (80% then 50% silver), and by late 1968 Canada's quarters were pure nickel. The decade also brought Canada's most celebrated coin: the 1967 Centennial Lynx.

196780%Ag

1967 Centennial — 80% Silver Commemorative

1867–196780% Silver

Canada's 100th anniversary. Reverse by artist Alex Colville features a bounding Canada lynx with dual dates "1867–1967." Machin portrait. Struck early in 1967 before the silver content was lowered.

Mintage: 48,855,500 combined (both alloys)

The 80% and 50% silver pieces are indistinguishable by eye; specific gravity testing or assay is required.

196750%Ag

1967 Centennial — 50% Silver Commemorative

1867–196750% Silver

Struck later in 1967 as rising silver prices forced the Mint to reduce silver content to 50%. Same lynx design by Alex Colville. Machin obverse with dual centennial dates.

Mintage: Included in 48,855,500 total

Transition to Nickel — Caribou 1968 • Two Issues

196850%Ag

1968 — 50% Silver (Early Strike)

196850% Silver

Small number struck early in 1968 in 50% silver before pure-nickel planchets were ready. Machin portrait, caribou reverse. Last silver Canadian quarter ever minted.

Mintage: ~71,464,000 silver portion (combined 1968 total ≈ 88.7M RCM + 85.2M Philadelphia)

Silver 1968 quarters are rare compared to the nickel issue. Look for edge colour — silver strikes are slightly brighter.

1968Ni

1968 — Pure Nickel (Main Issue)

196899.9% Nickel

First Canadian quarters struck in pure nickel. Machin tiara portrait, Hahn caribou reverse. Part of the 1968 run was struck at the Philadelphia Mint (no mint mark) to meet demand while Ottawa retooled. Nickel quarters are slightly lighter and less lustrous than silver.

Mintage: ≈ 88,686,931 (Ottawa) + 85,170,000 (Philadelphia) = 173,856,931 total
